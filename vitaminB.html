Things you need to know about Vitamin B and its various forms

Vitamin B is water soluble vitamin. It plays an important role in maintaining health of our body and immune system with other metabolic processes. Vitamin B composed of eight B vitamins ranging from B-1 to B-12 having many metabolic functions and benefits.Each of these essential component or vitamins contributes to overall functions of the body.



Benefit and sources of vitamin B1(thiamin)

Benefits of Vitamin B1

  • It uses in medicines
  • used for treatment of aids and cancer
  • helps to cure diabetic pain
  • helpful in motion sickness
  • uses to cure heart diseases
  • protect us from glaucoma and other vision problem

Sources of Vitamin B1

  • Cereals and pulses
  • Oranges
  • Eggs and yeast
  • Whole garin and nuts
  • Potatoes

Benefit and sources of vitamin B2(riboflavin)

Benefits of Vitamin B2

  • aids in energy production
  • helps regulate thyroid activity
  • maintaining healthy skin and eyes
  • boosts mineral abosorption in body
  • strengthens immune system

Sources of Vitamin B2

  • Fish, meat and poultry foods
  • Dairy products
  • Eggs
  • Avocados
  • Fortified cereals
  • Mushrooms
  • Beans and peas
  • Pumpkins
  • Sweet potatoes

Benefit and sources of vitamin B3(niacin)

Benefits of Vitamin B3

  • can help treat diabetes
  • maintain skin health
  • helps in imporving brain functions
  • can prevent erectile dysfunction
  • treats pellagra

Sources of Vitamin B3

  • Beef liver
  • Grilled chicken breast
  • Cooked brown rice
  • Dry roasted peanuts
  • Cereals
  • Sockeye salmon

Intake values of Vitamin B for men/women

  • B1(thiamin) 1.2 milligram(mg) per day
  • B2(riboflavin) 1.3 milligram(mg) per day
  • B3 (niacin) 16 milligram(mg) per day
  • B5 (pantothenic acid) 5 milligram(mg) per day
  • B6 (pyridoxine) 1.3 milligram(mg) per day
  • B7 (biotin) 30 microgram(mcg) per day
  • B9 (folic acid) 400 microgram(mcg) per day
  • B12 (cobalamin) 2.4 microgram(mcg) per day

Risk of over consuming

The consumption of vitamin B is common. Vitamin B is essential to maintain proper cell metabolism. It is a water soluble vitamin that is broken down by the body through digestion to promote proper body functioning. As every coin as two sides thus overdose of these vitamins may cause many problems such as skin rashes, gastrointestine problems and mood swings.
